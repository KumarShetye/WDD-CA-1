Effects of Climate Change

By Kumar Shetye



Threats to Nature and biodiversity



Climate change is affecting ecosystems worldwide, from pollinators like bees to forests and oceans. Rising temperatures, habitat loss, and pollution threaten the balance of nature that humans rely on for food, water, and clean air.





Immediate action is essential to protect biodiversity and maintain healthy ecosystems. By reducing emissions, conserving habitats, and supporting sustainable practices, we can ensure a more resilient planet for future generations.





The Rising Impact of Extreme Weather



Extreme weather events, such as wildfires, droughts, and floods, are becoming increasingly common due to climate change. These events not only destroy natural habitats but also impact human communities and agriculture.





Mitigating climate change requires both global and local efforts, from transitioning to renewable energy to restoring forests. Every action contributes to reducing risks and protecting both the environment and the people who depend on it.
